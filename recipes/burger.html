Burger

Description

A great homemade burger starts with a well-seasoned beef patty, cooked to juicy perfection and stacked high with fresh toppings. Simple, satisfying, and endlessly customisable.

This recipe focuses on getting the patty right — the right fat ratio, minimal handling, and a good hot sear. Once you nail the basics, you can build your perfect burger with whatever toppings you love.

Ingredients

  • 500g ground beef (20% fat)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 4 burger buns
  • 4 slices cheddar cheese
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • Lettuce leaves
  • 2 tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • Pickles
  • Ketchup and mustard, to serve
  • Mayonnaise, to serve

Steps

  1. Divide the ground beef into 4 equal portions. Gently shape each into a patty about 1.5cm thick. Press a small indent in the centre with your thumb to prevent puffing.
  2. Season both sides of each patty with salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
  3. Heat a cast iron pan or grill over high heat until very hot.
  4. Cook the patties for 3-4 minutes on the first side without pressing them down.
  5. Flip once and cook for another 3 minutes. Place a slice of cheddar on top and cook 1 more minute until melted.
  6. Remove patties and let rest for 2 minutes.
  7. Melt butter in the pan and toast the buns cut-side down for 1-2 minutes until golden.
  8. Spread mayonnaise, ketchup, and mustard on the buns.
  9. Layer lettuce, tomato, onion, and pickles on the bottom bun.
  10. Place the patty on top and close with the top bun. Serve immediately.
